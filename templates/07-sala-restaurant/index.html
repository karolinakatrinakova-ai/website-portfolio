A letter from the kitchen

We cook with what's local — and what the season brings

Sála is a forty-seat restaurant in a 1908 dining hall — the room kept its original tin ceiling, tile floor and wide windows looking onto Vinohradská. We took those four walls, a wood-burning grill, and a question we keep asking: what does Czech cooking taste like, here, now, when we use what's still in the ground?

Almost everything on the plate comes from inside a forty-kilometre radius. Vegetables from the Čermáks at Lhota since the spring of 2021. Pork and old-breed lamb from Statek u Cibulky. Cheese from the dairy at Ovocný Trh, eggs from the small farm in Kostelec. Whatever we cannot grow we ferment, smoke, dry, age — the larder runs the menu more than we do.

There is one tasting menu and a small à la carte; both change every two weeks. Wine is natural — almost entirely Moravian and Austrian — poured by people who know the growers. We are open six evenings a week and we close on Sundays and Mondays so the whole team has two days off in a row.

If you come, come hungry, stay long. We will keep the second pour of wine going.

— Anna & the Sála teamChef · co-owner · since 2021

The chef

Anna Hofmann cooks here every night

Born in Olomouc, trained in Vienna, two years at Frantzén in Stockholm and a year at Maaemo in Oslo. Anna came back to Prague in 2021 to open Sála — forty seats, an open kitchen, and a question about what modern Czech cooking could become.

She works the pass six nights a week, plates almost every dish that leaves the kitchen herself, and runs the menu as a single, stubborn point of view: a fortnight at a time, written by the season and the larder.
