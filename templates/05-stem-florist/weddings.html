Three weddings, three palettes

June 2025 · Mělník

Klára & Tomáš

A small garden wedding for 60 guests at a renovated farmhouse north of Prague. Klára wanted "what's blooming the day we get married, nothing else." We worked with what came in that week — peonies still tight, garden roses, the season's first sweet pea, and an absurd amount of lilac.

The ceremony arch was built foam-free on chicken wire and reused for the dinner head-table. Hand-tied bouquet, three bridesmaid posies, eight buttonholes. We delivered everything by 9am and stayed for set-up.

September 2025 · Praha

Anna & Jan

An autumn city wedding at Villa Pellé. Burgundy dahlia, terracotta amaranth, dried millet, copper beech — Anna sent a single Pinterest pin and said "make it look picked, not made." We did.

Three full venue installations (entrance arch, ceremony backdrop, dinner ceiling), thirty taper candles, and a cake table with chocolate cosmos. The bouquet weighed exactly the right amount and Anna held it correctly in zero photos.

May 2025 · Karlštejn forest

Eva & Marek

An elopement in a Karlštejn forest clearing. Eight guests, a lot of moss, exactly one violin. We brought a single oversized bouquet, two boutonnieres, and a small ground-laid arrangement around the wooden ceremony plinth. Then we went home.

Sweet pea, white peonies, fern, lily of the valley still cool from the morning. The bouquet was so loose it shed petals every step she took. That was, mostly, the point.
