Four ways back to your body

Every class on the schedule is one of four shapes — vinyasa, yin, pilates or breath. Read what each actually feels like, who it's for, and what to bring. None of them require flexibility, only patience.

Vinyasa · 60 — 75 min

Move with the breath

Vinyasa — flow, not flexibility

A flowing class where each movement matches a breath. Slow Flow is gentle and grounded; Open Flow is steady; Power Flow is what most people call a "real workout". You'll sweat. You'll also leave calmer than you came.

If you've never done yoga, start with Slow Flow or Sunrise. The teacher will offer modifications for every shape. There's no test, no goal, no Instagram pose. Just twelve people on twelve mats, breathing.

Anyone

Yin · 75 min

Stay, soften, surrender

Yin — for the quieter body

Long, supported holds (3 — 5 minutes per shape) into the deeper, slower tissues. Almost no movement. A lot of waiting. People often think they're "doing nothing" — and then they sleep better than they have in months.

Bring a sweater; the room cools while you stay still. We use bolsters, blocks and blankets — provided. Yin pairs well with desk-jobs, marathons, anxiety, and grief.

Pilates · 60 min

Strong middle, kind body

Pilates — strength without the drama

Mat-based pilates focused on the deep core, glutes, and posture. Mat Pilates is foundational; Strong Pilates layers in resistance bands and intensity. Lucia teaches like a physio — slow, precise, and zero ego.

If you have a back, knee or shoulder thing, this class will help (and Lucia will modify on the spot). Most people leave standing taller. We use no machines — just bodies, mats, and the occasional band.

Breath · 30 — 60 min

The shortest path to calmer

Breath — the underrated class

Pranayama and breath-work — sometimes seated, sometimes lying down, occasionally with sound. Lunch Breath (30 min) is gentle and clears the head. Sunday Breath (60 min) goes deeper, with longer holds and a real reset.

If you've ever cried in a yoga class and wondered what happened — that was probably the breath. This is a good place to come if everything's a bit much, or if you can't sit still long enough to "meditate". You don't have to.
