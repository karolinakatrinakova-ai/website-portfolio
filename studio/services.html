Services

What I can help you with.

Honest scope. I do these things, and I do them with care.

  1. 01

    Designing your website

    A look that fits your business — calm, considered, on brand. We sketch ideas together over a couple of conversations and refine until it feels right. No moodboards forced on you, no design by committee.

    Good for: a brand-new website, or a redesign of something tired.

  2. 02

    Building it by hand

    Hand-coded HTML, CSS and JavaScript. No Wix, no Squarespace, no monthly platform tax. The site is fast, lightweight, and you own everything — including the code.

    Good for: businesses who want a real website without recurring software fees.

  3. 03

    Writing the words

    If writing about your own business feels strange, I'll draft the copy for your home page in plain, warm language. I bring a marketing background — I know what tends to land and what tends to feel like noise.

    Good for: people who'd rather not stare at a blank Google Doc.

  4. 04

    Helping people find you locally

    Setting up your Google Map listing, optimising the basics on your website (meta tags, page titles, simple structure), and making sure your address, opening hours and contact info are consistent everywhere.

    Good for: businesses where most clients live nearby — cafés, salons, clinics, studios.

  5. 05

    EN + SK + CS toggle

    If your customers come from across the border or include English-speaking visitors, I can build a clean language switcher into your site. Same content, three languages, one click. Just like this website.

    Good for: border-region businesses, B&Bs, photographers, anyone in tourism.

  6. 06

    Small monthly check-ups (optional)

    If you'd like, I can keep an eye on your website each month — minor edits, fresh photos, content updates, small tweaks. No commitment, no minimum term. We just talk and figure out what's useful.

    Good for: businesses that change often (menus, schedules, prices) and want a real human to ask.

Honesty

What I don't do (yet)

I'm a small studio and I'm just starting. I'd rather say no than promise something I can't deliver well. So:

  • — I don't build large e-shops or complex booking systems. For that, you'll be better off with a specialist.
  • — I don't run paid ads or social-media management as a service. (I can advise from my Kofio days, but it's not what I sell.)
  • — I don't promise number-one rankings on Google. Anyone who does is selling you something.
  • — I don't work in 24-hour turnarounds. I take the time a project deserves.

Sound like a fit?

Write to me — even just to say hi. We'll see if it makes sense.
